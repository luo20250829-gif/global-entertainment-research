Regulatory Trends & Frameworks

How governments across emerging markets are shaping the online entertainment industry

The Regulatory Spectrum

Online entertainment regulation across our four focus markets spans a wide spectrum — from the Philippines' well-established PAGCOR licensing system to Indonesia's more restrictive Kominfo-led approach. Understanding where each market sits on this spectrum, and where it is heading, is essential for operators, investors, and researchers evaluating market opportunities.

A detailed 20-country regulatory comparison, including tax rates, licensing requirements, and enforcement patterns, is published in the Global Regulatory Landscape 2026 report on Entertain Monitor.

Country Regulatory Snapshots

India

Federal-state hybrid model. 28% GST upheld judicially. 18 states with varying stances. Self-regulatory organizations (SROs) emerging as industry-led compliance bodies. The "skill vs. chance" legal distinction remains the central regulatory question.

Indonesia

Restrictive approach under Kominfo. Active URL blocking (thousands of sites blocked 2020-2025). No formal licensing framework for most online entertainment categories. E-sports recognized through PBESI. Regulatory clarity is the market's biggest challenge.

Full Indonesia analysis →

Philippines

Most mature framework in the group. PAGCOR administers four license types with clear capitalization and technology requirements. Post-POGO ban reshaping has refocused regulation on domestic market protection and operator quality standards.

Full Philippines analysis →

Mexico

SEGOB federal licensing with state-level overlay. Established land-based framework adapting to online. Growing emphasis on AML compliance and responsible use. Regional comparison with Colombia, Argentina, Brazil, and Peru reveals divergent Latin American approaches.

Emerging Trends

Taxation convergence. Across markets, governments are moving toward dedicated entertainment/gaming taxes. India's 28% GST, discussions in the Philippines about rate adjustments, and Mexico's evolving tax treatment all point toward a global normalization of industry-specific taxation.

Responsible use mandates. Self-exclusion tools, deposit limits, and time-out features are increasingly becoming regulatory requirements rather than voluntary best practices. India's SRO framework and PAGCOR's updated compliance standards both reflect this trend.

Data localization. Indonesia's Government Regulation 71 and India's proposed data protection framework are pushing platforms toward local data storage and processing, with implications for operational costs and system architecture.
